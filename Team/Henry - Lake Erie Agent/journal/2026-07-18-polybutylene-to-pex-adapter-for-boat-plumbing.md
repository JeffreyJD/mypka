# Happy Ours' original fresh-water plumbing is polybutylene, not PEX — the transition needs a specific "Polybutylene Adapter" barb fitting, not a standard PEX coupling

## Context
Jeffrey repaired/upgraded the head's water supply connection on Happy Ours (2002 Sea Ray 340) on 2026-07-18, transitioning the boat's original polybutylene supply line to new 1/2" PEX tubing.

## What I learned
Older boats (and homes/RVs of the same era) were commonly plumbed with polybutylene (PB) tubing, which was phased out industry-wide in the late 1990s due to failure issues but is still present in plenty of vessels this age. PB and PEX are NOT the same OD/dimension standard, and a generic PEX-to-PEX barb coupling will not seal reliably on a PB line. The fix is a purpose-made **polybutylene-to-PEX transition adapter** — in this case the **Apollo PEX 1/2" x 1/2" Polybutylene Adapter (barb fitting), item #277-486** — which is dimensioned to grip PB tubing on one side and PEX barb on the other. This is the part to reach for any time a poly line on Happy Ours needs to be extended, repaired, or converted to PEX, not a standard PEX coupling.

## When this applies
Any future repair/upgrade of Happy Ours' fresh-water plumbing (head, galley, washdown, generator raw-water) where the existing run is the original polybutylene tubing and the plan is to extend or convert to PEX. Also applies generally to any other pre-2000s vessel or fixture with polybutylene plumbing.

## When this does NOT apply
Does not apply to copper, PVC, or already-converted PEX-to-PEX runs — those use standard couplings. Does not apply to raw-water (through-hull/seawater) lines, which use marine hose and hose clamps, not PEX/PB fittings.
